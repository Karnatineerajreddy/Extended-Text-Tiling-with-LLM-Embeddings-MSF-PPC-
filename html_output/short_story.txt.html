The sun had just begun to sink behind the distant tree line when Mia arrived at the old cabin she had not visited since childhood, and although years had passed and countless memories had faded, the quiet stillness of the place triggered a flood of emotions that felt both distant and strangely familiar, as if the past were gently calling her back.

As she stepped inside, the wooden floorboards creaked beneath her feet while dust particles drifted lazily through the slivers of golden evening light, creating an atmosphere that was both nostalgic and unsettling, making the once-warm cabin feel colder and more mysterious than she remembered.

When a sudden noise echoed from the back room, Mia froze in place, her heart racing uncontrollably as her curiosity battled with her fear, and although she eventually gathered the courage to push open the door and discovered only a rocking chair swaying gently in the draft, the fresh footprints on the floor made it clear that someone else had been there—and possibly still was.
